## Overview

Spain exports **$1.17 billion USD** annually to the Dominican Republic across 2,338 product categories. Spain is DR's **5th largest import partner** and **2nd largest foreign investor** ($1.126B FDI in 2024). Over **100 Spanish companies** operate in DR generating ~29,000 jobs. Bilateral trade has grown **83% since 2012**.

This corridor is active, large, and growing — and it's exactly where Qargo operates.

## Section 3: Logistics Pain Points (Your Sales Pitch)

These are the documented, real frustrations Spanish exporters face on the Spain → DR route. Use these in every conversation:

### 1. Customs Documentation Errors (Most Common Pain)
DR's SIGA customs system requires bill of lading to include shipper/consignee tax ID or passport number for all parties. Many Spanish exporters miss this → cargo held at port, delays, unexpected costs.

**Qargo solves:** We prepare all documentation before shipment leaves Spain.

### 2. Transit Time and No Visibility
Sea transit Spain → DR: 15–25 days with poor visibility on cargo status.

**Qargo solves:** Real-time tracking and proactive updates throughout transit.

### 3. LCL (Small Shipment) Problems
Spanish SMEs often ship LCL (groupage) because they don't fill a full container. Result: cargo sits waiting for the container to fill, extending transit by days or weeks. More expensive per unit. Higher damage risk from multiple handling.

**Qargo solves:** We consolidate multiple clients' cargo for weekly sailings, reducing cost and transit time.

### 4. Product Registration Complexity
Pharmaceuticals, cosmetics, and food products require registration with DR authorities (DIGEMAPS for food, Ministry of Public Health for pharma). Process is slow and expensive without experienced local agents.

**Qargo solves:** We manage the full registration process with local agents.

### 5. Port Congestion Dec–April
Caucedo port faces peak congestion during high tourist season and harvest periods.

**Qargo solves:** We schedule shipments to avoid peak periods and have contingency routing through Rio Haina.

### 6. Non-Standard Customs Valuation
DR customs officials sometimes apply different valuation methods than declared. Without an experienced local broker, companies get surprise import duties.

**Qargo solves:** We have established customs broker relationships and handle disputes proactively.

### 7. Last-Mile to Final Destination
Once cargo clears customs in Santo Domingo, getting it to final destination — especially outside the capital — involves poor roads, unreliable local carriers, and no tracking.

**Qargo solves:** We own the last-mile. Door-to-door delivery across DR.
