# DR Market Research Report
## Dominican Republic — Logistics & Trade Intelligence
*Compiled March 2026*

---

## Executive Summary

The Dominican Republic sits at a rare intersection of opportunity: a $12.9B export economy growing 5% annually, a $32B import base, a free zone system generating $8.6B in manufactured exports, and an e-commerce sector hitting $5.2B with a 17% CAGR. The country is simultaneously the world's largest organic cacao exporter, the #7 medical device exporter to the US, the global premium cigar capital, and the fastest-growing nearshoring hub in the Caribbean.

For Qargo — a logistics orchestrator, not an asset owner — this creates multiple high-value corridors where intelligent intermediation is the structurally superior position.

| Spain | 1.0% (**growing fast**) |
| Belgium | 0.93% (cocoa) |

### The Europe Corridor — Underserved and Booming
- Europe receives ~8-10% of DR exports — but growing
- Cocoa to Spain **+359%** in one year
- Netherlands, Germany, Belgium are top cocoa/food buyers
- **This corridor is structurally undersupported by logistics** relative to its growth rate
- EU sustainability certifications create premium pricing but require sophisticated logistics

### The Caribbean/Regional Corridor
- Haiti: 6.9% of DR exports (largely informal)
- Jamaica + Caribbean islands: ~2-3% combined
- Intra-Caribbean trade is fragmented — major opportunity for an intermediary

### Port Infrastructure
- **Port Caucedo** (DP World): 1.7M TEUs (2024); expanding to 3.1M TEU ($760M investment — MOU signed May 2025)
- **Rio Haina:** Traditional containerized cargo port
- DR explicitly positioning itself as the **transshipment hub of the Caribbean**

## Section 4: E-Commerce and Small Packages

### Market Size
- **2024 market:** $5.2 billion
- **CAGR through 2027:** 17% (projected to reach $8.3B)
- **Cross-border e-commerce 2025 growth:** 22%

### Consumer Behavior
- 70% of adults shop online
- 82% of online shoppers use WhatsApp as their access point
- 52% of Dominicans buy FMCG online (vs 10% regional average)
- 8.7 million online banking users (85% of adults 15+)

### Small Package Opportunity
- Dominant inbound: US → DR via "casillero" (Miami mailbox) model
- Main players: iPlus (#1 by traffic), AEROPAQ, EPS, BM Cargo
- **Gap: outbound DR → international is completely unclaimed**
- DR artisans, cosmetics SMEs, food producers selling direct-to-consumer abroad have no reliable shipping solution

## Section 5: Priority Target Sectors for Qargo

### Priority 1: Organic Cacao Exporters
**Why:** $692.5M growing 25%/year. EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR) requires chain-of-custody documentation that no current DR logistics provider handles. DR has 70% of global organic cacao.

**Key targets:** CONACADO, YACAO, OKO Caribe, Rizek Cacao, Santiago Cocoa

**Qargo angle:** Position as the "EU-compliant export orchestrator" — digital tracking + EUDR documentation + competitive freight to Europe.

---

### Priority 2: Cosmetics / Personal Care SMEs
**Why:** 347 registered companies, $144.6M in exports growing at 12.6%. These companies make the product but have zero export logistics knowledge. Massively underexporting their potential.

**Key contact:** AFAPER (Dominican cosmetics association) — one partnership reaches all 347 members

**Qargo angle:** Standard export logistics package (customs + courier rates + documentation) sold through the association.

---

### Priority 3: Boutique Cigar Exporters (Non-US Routes)
**Why:** $1.34B sector, 145+ countries. US route ($1B, 74%) is well-served. South Africa, Australia, Poland, Japan, emerging Europe — wide open. Hand-rolled cigars need humidity/temperature control that generic couriers can't provide.

**Key contact:** Instituto del Tabaco Dominicano (In-tabaco)

**Qargo angle:** Specialize in "rest of world" cigar routing with temperature-controlled handling.

---

### Priority 4: Outbound Small Parcel (DR → International)
**Why:** Inbound courier market is crowded. Outbound is 100% unclaimed. 70% of adults shop online, WhatsApp commerce exploding, micro-entrepreneurs need international shipping.

**Qargo angle:** "Ship from Dominican Republic" product — aggregated rates from DHL/FedEx/couriers, simple booking, documentation handling.

---

### Priority 5: Free Zone Mid-Tier Manufacturers (Non-US Routes)
**Why:** 850 companies, 72% export to US (well-served). 28% = ~$2.4B going to Europe/Caribbean/LATAM with no dominant intermediary. Caucedo expansion will bring 50-100 new companies in 2 years.

**Key contacts:** ADOZONA, ProIndustria, free zone park operators in Santiago

**Qargo angle:** "Non-US export desk" — freight rates, customs docs, compliance filing as a managed service.

## Section 6: Competitive Landscape

### Large Carriers (Not Competitors — They Are Partners)
- **DP World Caucedo** — port operator, no SME services
- **Maersk, CMA CGM, Hapag-Lloyd** — ocean freight only
- **Crowley** — US-centric, limited intra-Caribbean/Europe routing
- **DHL, FedEx, UPS** — corporate accounts, expensive for SMEs

### Domestic Couriers (Inbound Focus)
- **iPlus** — #1 in DR (398K monthly visits), inbound US→DR only, no export expertise
- **AEROPAQ** — air courier from USA, expensive, no sea freight
- **EPS** — traditional operator, no vertical specialization
- **BM Cargo** — general freight forwarder

### The Gap Qargo Owns
Nobody owns:
- Export logistics for DR's high-growth SME sectors (cacao, cosmetics, cigars, coffee)
- Last-mile door-to-door for the full country
- Intra-Caribbean B2B freight orchestration
- EU-compliant documentation for DR agricultural exports
